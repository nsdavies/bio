Pokemon Game

I created a Pokemon Game my 1st year at Oregon State. This was a very simple Pokemon game at first. A 9X9 grid that a player was in and the whole goal of really any Pokemon game is to catch em all. There was only about 12 total Pokemon that the user needed to catch. The user caught them by throwing pokeballs at them until it was caught with each having a different catch rate. On the grid, there were at least 2 caves that were generated which would give a player pokeballs and a moonstone at random. The player would use the moonstone to evolve there pokemon until it reached its final evolution (yeah I know that is not how every Pokemon evolves but that was the requirement and trust me I strongly objected against it). Once the player caught and evolved every Pokemon in the game, the player returned to the starting tile where Professor Oak was and that ended the game. Part of the extra credit was to make a dumb AI that would play the game and eventually win which was super fun to write. It is a extremely diluted version of Pokemon but it was my favorite assignment all-time to create becuase I loved the challenge and I sure do love Pokemon.
